**Nova Patch** (they/them), Dublin, Ireland.

I work at the intersection of language, technology, and culture.

My background is in software engineering and product development. I’m interested in how global systems are built: internationalization, character encoding, typography, search, and metadata all shape everyday experience across languages.

That work has touched more than 30 languages through product localization, open source development, and contributions to open standards, including longtime involvement with the Unicode Consortium. Accessible digital infrastructure and inclusive design remain central concerns.

I’m also drawn to systems of observation and taxonomy in natural history, particularly lichenology, ecological surveying, camera trapping, and acoustic logging. The same questions return there: how we name things, how we classify them, how technology extends observation, and how open data helps communities understand ecosystems.

This site is a *digital garden* for ongoing work.
